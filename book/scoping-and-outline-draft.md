## What this is for

A possible **technical monograph** (~80-150 manuscript pages) on the SPARRING Framework, targeting an audience that is reached neither by the academic-research route (arXiv, workshop venues) nor by the LinkedIn casual-scroll layer:

- **Casual business / micro-business decision-makers** who buy technical books to learn frameworks they can apply (the *Continuous Discovery Habits* / *High Output Management* audience).
- **Agentic-AI enthusiasts** -- practitioners and curious-builders who follow Latent Space, Eugene Yan, Hamel Husain, Simon Willison's blog, the AI Substack ecosystem.
- **Practitioner-implementers** who want a usable methodology, not a reference manual or a peer-review preprint.

This audience treats a published book as a credibility signal in a way they don't treat a GitHub repo, and they will buy a $25-35 technical book on a topic they're curious about. They will not read an arXiv PDF and will not pip-install a reference implementation, but they will read a 100-page narrative-led book with worked examples.

The format-and-audience parallel that's closest is **Teresa Torres's *Continuous Discovery Habits*** -- self-published, ~250 pages, framework-led narrative, sells steadily, gets adopted as a methodology by readers, and is exactly the audience Series 2 of the LinkedIn series is going to compare SPARRING against. Same readers.

## Why narrative-led, not reference-manual

Two shapes the work could take:

| Shape | Result | Who finishes it |
|---|---|---|
| Reference manual (Part I "The Problem" / Part II "The Framework" / Part III "Building" / Part IV "Using") | Stitched-document feel, even if technically complete | Practitioners use it as a lookup; the casual-business audience does not finish |
| Narrative-led (story -> problem -> framework -> worked example -> reflection) | Coherent journey | Casual-business + agentic-AI-enthusiast audience reads through |

The narrative-led shape is the one that reaches the audience this book is for. The reference-manual content already exists in `framework/professional-distribution/`; the *book* is the narrative scaffolding around it.

Closest format templates: Torres *Continuous Discovery Habits*; Eric Evans *Domain-Driven Design*; Andy Hunt + Dave Thomas *The Pragmatic Programmer*.

## Critique of the ChatGPT-generated assembly proposal (2026-05-03)

A separate proposal generated by a different chat assistant (working from slightly stale source docs) recommended a "structured reassembly + light normalization pass" with a "no content expansion" rule. **The proposal's production skeleton is mostly right; the book it plans is for the wrong audience.**

What to keep from that proposal:
- Pandoc pipeline, single-canonical-`.md` source, PDF + epub output formats.
- Four-act high-level scaffold (problem -> framework -> implementation -> practice) as the underlying logic, even if surface organization is journey-shaped.
- "Preserve terminology exactly" rule -- SPARK / Pattern Lock / The Cut / PNP / Generator / Challenger / Nine Disciplines are framework identity anchors, must not drift.
- Move CLI commands and config schemas to appendix.
- Marcus persona example, disagreement-at-cap protocol, discipline definitions, failure-mode taxonomy must remain intact.
- De-duplication discipline ("keep strongest version, remove weaker duplicates").

What to revise:
- **Audience.** ChatGPT proposal targeted "technically savvy developers new to agentic systems" -- a narrower audience already served by the implementation guide. The book targets a broader practitioner + casual-business + AI-enthusiast audience.
- **"No content expansion" rule.** Forbids exactly the writing that distinguishes a book from a stitched document set: opening hooks, between-chapter transitions, author's voice / origin story, worked-example narratives, reflective closing chapters. The narrative *is* expansion.
- **Empirical chapter is missing.** ChatGPT's outline has no chapter for the V1 pilot story (pre-registered gate, gate failed at p = 0.351 vs. 0.4 threshold, partner-anchor inversion, pack-fidelity finding). This is the strongest material in the book -- honest "we tried this and it failed in an instructive way" content is exactly what this audience responds to.
- **V2 missing entirely.** Writing a methodology book about a pre-registered hypothesis you haven't yet tested is the wrong sequence. Write *through* V2 results, including whatever V2 finds.
- **Stale source paths.** ChatGPT references files as flat docs; they now live at `framework/professional-distribution/` and the V1 preprint lives at `pilots/llm-judge-2026-05-02/v1/preprint-draft.md`.
- **No byline / ORCID / register treatment.** Per the byline spar of 2026-05-03: book authored as **Barton Niedner / Resource Forge LLC**, ORCID iD on copyright page. LinkedIn promotion is under "Bart Niedner" (business voice).
- **No versioning discipline.** Book = frozen v1.0 snapshot; GitHub repo = living current state. Need an explicit "what's changed since this book was published" pointer (likely `framework/professional-distribution/post-publication-notes.md`) for future-readers.
- **No distribution / launch.** ChatGPT proposal stops at pandoc compile. Need KDP setup, Leanpub option, ISBN, cover, launch sequence aligned with the LinkedIn series.
- **Decision-frameworks comparison missing.** Series 2 LinkedIn material (Torres / CDH / OST) is the natural "how this fits with what you already know" chapter for a practitioner audience.
- **Reading time claim.** "3-5 hours total" at 80-100 pages of technical content is wrong by 2-3x. Real technical reads at that length are 8-15 hours. (Not load-bearing for the plan, but a sign the proposal author didn't fully think through the medium.)

## Versioning discipline

- The book is a **frozen v1.0 snapshot** of the framework as of the publication date.
- The GitHub repo (`muddyone/sparring-framework`) is the **living current state**; framework continues to evolve.

- **Future editions** track major framework revisions (e.g., a 2nd edition after V3 or a successor pilot).

## Open questions

1. **Validate Chapter 1 voice before committing to the structure.** Lowest-stakes way to test whether the narrative-led format reaches the target audience: draft Chapter 1 (~6 pp, ~1 hour of writing) and read it cold. The voice and shape either land or don't, and a Chapter 1 read tells you more than ten more pages of structural debate.

2. **Confirm V2 timeline before locking publication date.** V2 is the load-bearing dependency for Chapter 7. If V2 slips, the book slips. If V2 finds the methodology gate fails again, the book's empirical chapter is materially different (and arguably more interesting -- a methodology that has documented its own failure modes twice and proposed instrumented next steps is rarer and more useful than a triumphalist book that hasn't been pressure-tested).

3. **Decide between "single-author monograph" vs. "Niedner with named contributors."** The V1 pilot involved Matthew Niedner as a partner-rater; some of the pack-fidelity finding traces to his rating session. Acknowledgments vs. co-author depends on Matthew's preference and the depth of his contribution to the manuscript itself (vs. the underlying empirical work). Defer to manuscript-readiness conversation.

4. **ISBN: pay for one or use KDP-free.** Free KDP-issued ISBN constrains distribution to KDP only; paid Bowker ISBN allows broader distribution (including Leanpub, IngramSpark, libraries, indie bookstores). $125 is not load-bearing on the budget; the question is whether broader-than-KDP distribution matters for this audience. Likely yes, given the book wants to reach the practitioner and academic-curious audiences as well.

5. **Whether to do a Leanpub in-progress release before the manuscript is final.** Leanpub specifically supports "buy a book that's still being written; receive updates as new chapters land." Pairs well with the V2-in-flight timing. Risk: split attention between manuscript writing and Leanpub-customer feedback management. Benefit: early audience signal on whether the format / voice is working before the print version is committed. Defer until Chapter 1 voice validation lands.
